Palestinian Arabic Greetings

Greetings are the best place to start learning Palestinian Arabic because they appear in almost every conversation. Instead of memorizing only single words, practice short phrases you can say naturally when meeting someone.

Advertisement

Useful Greeting Situations

Begin with hello, welcome, how are you, good morning, good evening, thank you, and polite replies. These phrases help you sound warmer and more natural when speaking with Palestinians or other Levantine Arabic speakers.

Study Tip

Say each greeting out loud, then use it in a tiny dialogue. Palestinian Arabic pronunciation becomes easier when you repeat whole phrases with rhythm, not isolated vocabulary only.
