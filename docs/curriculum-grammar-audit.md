## Main Findings

1. The course is strong in functional vocabulary and real-life dialogue.
2. Grammar is usually embedded in hints and examples rather than presented as a visible progression.
3. The largest structural gaps are passive participles and human/non-human agreement.
4. Dual, plural systems, past conjugation, adjective agreement, and calendar dates need explicit consolidation.
5. Beginner and Pre-Intermediate units need more controlled exercises before open speaking tasks.

## Exercise Upgrade

The first supplemental Grammar Lab adds 40 activities across Family, Daily Routine, Shopping, Plans & Future, and Feelings. Its activity types mirror the supplied references:

- Translate into Gaza Palestinian Arabic.
- Correct the sentence.
- Fill in the correct conjugation.
- Fill in the suitable verb or grammar form.

The next content phase should add explicit grammar cards/tables for every Partial or Missing topic, starting with past conjugation, plural systems, passive participles, and human/non-human agreement.
