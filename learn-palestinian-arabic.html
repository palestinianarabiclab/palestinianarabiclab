Learn Palestinian Arabic Online

Palestinian Arabic is the everyday spoken Arabic used in Palestine and by many Palestinian communities around the world. It is part of the wider Levantine Arabic family, but it has its own rhythm, vocabulary, expressions, and warm conversational style. This site is built for learners who want practical language they can understand and use, not only lists of isolated words.

The free lesson app focuses on beginner-friendly topics: greetings, introductions, family, food and drink, daily routines, shopping, health, work, feelings, opinions, and future plans. Each topic includes vocabulary, short dialogues, grammar notes, translation practice, and review activities that help you move from recognition to speaking.

A Practical Study Path

A good first goal is to learn small phrases that you can reuse. For example, instead of memorizing only the word for "name", learn a full expression like "What is your name?" and practice answering it. After that, change one part of the sentence: where you are from, where you live, what you study, what you like, or what you want to do next.

Start with listening and repeating. Then read the Arabic line slowly, check the meaning, and say the phrase again without looking. This simple cycle helps with pronunciation, memory, and confidence. The lessons are short on purpose so you can repeat them several times instead of rushing through too much new material.

What Makes Palestinian Arabic Different?

Palestinian Arabic uses many expressions that sound natural in daily conversation. Learners often notice differences between textbook Arabic and spoken Arabic, especially in verbs, question words, and everyday greetings. This site explains those patterns gently, with examples that are useful for real conversations with friends, family members, teachers, or people you meet while traveling.
