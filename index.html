Free Palestinian Arabic Lessons for Beginners

Palestinian Arabic Lab is a free learning resource for students who want to understand spoken Palestinian Arabic in real situations. The lessons focus on useful phrases, short dialogues, vocabulary, pronunciation, grammar patterns, and practice tasks.

What You Can Study

Start with greetings and introductions, then move into family, food and drink, daily routines, shopping, health, work, feelings, opinions, and future plans. Each lesson is designed to help you recognize common Palestinian expressions and use them in simple conversations.

Real-Life Dialogues

Practice Palestinian Arabic through everyday situations: ordering at a restaurant, visiting family, taking transportation, welcoming guests, and explaining a health problem to a doctor. These scenarios help learners move from vocabulary lists into practical conversations they can actually use.

Who This Site Is For

This site is for Arabic learners, heritage learners, travelers, language teachers, and anyone interested in spoken Palestinian Arabic. You can browse the lessons without creating an account and book a trial lesson when you want guided practice.

How the Lessons Are Organized

The course is arranged around real communication tasks instead of isolated grammar lists. Learners begin with greetings and personal introductions, then practice family, food, daily routine, shopping, health, work and study, feelings, opinions, and plans. Each topic includes vocabulary, short examples, dialogue practice, and review activities so learners can return to the same material more than once.

Original Learning Material

The examples on Palestinian Arabic Lab are written for this project and focus on spoken Palestinian Arabic. The goal is to help learners notice natural sentence patterns, common replies, and practical expressions they can reuse with teachers, friends, family members, and people they meet while traveling.

For longer reading guides, visit the pages on learning Palestinian Arabic, beginner study steps, useful phrases, greetings, and pronunciation. You can also review family vocabulary, daily conversation examples, and the difference between Palestinian Arabic and MSA.
